What are pulsars?

Pulsars are the stellar remains of massive stars. They are formed when the core of a star is compressed during a supernova forming a neutron star. This type of neutron star actually maintains the angular momentum of the original star, however it only a fraction of the size which causes its high rotation speed. Pulsars get their name from the beam of radiation that is emitted along the magnetic axis of the neutron star which acts like a stellar lighthouse due to the neutron star's rotation.


Pulsars are so dense that a matchbox of material would have an approximate mass of 3 billion tonnes!
